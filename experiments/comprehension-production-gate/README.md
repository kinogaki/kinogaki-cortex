# Exp AY — two-threshold comprehension/production gate (the C>P lag organ, M17)

A child understands a word before it will say it. This experiment reads ONE leaky word-binding count at
two operating points: comprehension opens when any incoming binding (a context→word count) clears a LOW
threshold; production opens only when the word is its context's ARGMAX and the context's NARS truth f·c
(AB's hit/miss split) clears a HIGH bar. Tracking a CDI-like watch-set of mid-frequency words over a
single online pass of a text8 slice (CHILDES substituted — see RESULTS), the production onset lands
later than the comprehension onset (the single-threshold baseline gives exactly zero lag), and the lag
widens with competitor density (AO fan) on both seeds — "understands but won't say it yet," worse for
confusable words. Run: `python exp_ay_cpgate/run.py`.
